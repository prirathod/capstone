How to Open a Bank Account

  • Provide ID: Bring two forms of ID, such as a passport, driver's license, letter of acceptance, or study permit.
  • Provide Proof of Enrolment: If you're over 23, you'll need to provide proof of enrolment in a post-secondary program.
  • Choose a Financial Institution: Consider the bank's location, services, interest rates, and service charges.
  • Open the Account: You can open an account in person, by phone, or electronically.

List of Banks in Edmonton

  1. Royal Bank of Canada (RBC): One of Canada's largest banks, offering personal and business banking services.
  2. TD Canada Trust: A major bank offering individuals and businesses a wide range of financial products and services.
  3. Scotiabank: A global bank providing personal banking, wealth management, and business banking solutions.
  4. Bank of Montreal (BMO): A leading bank in Canada offering personal and commercial banking services.
  5. CIBC (Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce): A major Canadian bank with a full suite of banking products, including loans, mortgages, and investment services.
  6. National Bank of Canada: Provides a range of personal, commercial, and wealth management services.
  7. ATB Financial: A provincial bank in Alberta that provides a wide variety of banking services, including personal and business accounts.
  8. Servus Credit Union: A credit union based in Alberta, offering a full suite of financial products and services to its members.
  9. Desjardins: Although more prominent in Quebec, Desjardins operates in Alberta as well, offering cooperative banking services.
  10. HSBC Canada: A global bank with a presence in Edmonton, offering international banking services.
