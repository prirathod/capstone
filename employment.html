Employment Rules for International Students in Canada

International students in Canada can work on-campus and off-campus under specific conditions based on their study permit. Below are the detailed guidelines:

1. On-Campus Jobs

  • No Restrictions on Hours (During Study Periods): Students can work unlimited hours on-campus while enrolled full-time at a designated learning institution (DLI).
  • During Breaks: Students can work full-time (more than 20 hours per week) during scheduled breaks such as winter holidays, summer vacation, or spring break.
  • On-Campus Job Definition: Includes work in campus facilities such as libraries, cafeterias, or as a research assistant.

2. Off-Campus Jobs

  • Up to 20 Hours per Week (During Academic Terms): Students can work up to 20 hours per week off-campus during regular academic sessions.
  • Full-Time Hours During Breaks: Students can work full-time during scheduled breaks as long as they are enrolled as full-time students in the current or upcoming semester.
  • Eligibility: Students must have a valid study permit and be enrolled in a program of at least 6 months leading to a recognized credential (degree, diploma, or certificate).

Additional Points to Consider

  • Co-op and Internships: Programs that require co-ops or internships allow students to work full-time during the academic term with a co-op work permit. These hours are not part of the 20-hour weekly limit.
  • Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP): After completing studies, students may apply for a PGWP, allowing full-time work for up to 3 years based on the length of their study program.
  • Student Eligibility: Students must be enrolled full-time in a DLI program and meet IRCC work criteria.

Summary

  • On-Campus Jobs: No specific limit during academic terms; full-time during breaks.
  • Off-Campus Jobs: Up to 20 hours per week during academic sessions; full-time during scheduled breaks.
