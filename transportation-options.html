Public Transit

1. Edmonton Transit System (ETS)

  • Service: Buses and LRTs cover the city.
  • U-Pass: MacEwan students get a free, unlimited U-Pass for bus and LRT use, included in tuition fees.
  • How to Get It: Automatically issued when enrolled at MacEwan University.

2. Additional Passes

  • Single Tickets: Buy from vending machines or the ETS app.
  • Monthly Passes: Available if you need more than the U-Pass.

3. Payment

  • Methods: Use credit/debit cards or the ETS app to secure payments.

For more details, check the ETS website or visit their customer service.

Car Service

Taxis or Ubers are available for short one-way trips. Uber is a ride-sharing service that is an alternative to public transit. If you prefer to drive yourself and you have a valid driver's license in Canada, try Communauto.

Communauto is a car-share transportation option that allows you to borrow a car for short trips.

Other Options

  • Walking and Biking: Always an option for shorter trips.
  • E-Bikes and E-Scooters: Rentals are available on many downtown street corners in the summer and fall.
  • Bike Grid: The downtown Edmonton area features a convenient bike grid.
